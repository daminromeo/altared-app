# Backlink & Authority Checklist — altared.app

A working tracker for earning backlinks to raise domain authority and crawl
budget (the current ceiling on indexing). Quality and relevance beat volume.
**Never** buy links, use PBNs, or "1,000 backlinks" services — for a young
domain that risks a penalty before you ever rank.

Status key: `[ ]` todo · `[~]` in progress · `[x]` done. Add the live URL when done.

---

- [ ] **BetaList** — https://betalist.com/submit

- [ ] **Crunchbase** — company profile. https://crunchbase.com
- [ ] **SaaSHub / SaaSworthy / Capterra / G2** — software directories (do-follow on some).
- [ ] **Indie Hackers** — product page + build-in-public posts. https://indiehackers.com

### Wedding-specific directories & tool lists
- [ ] Get listed as a **planning tool** on wedding resource/round-up pages couples browse.
- [ ] Reach out to "best wedding budget apps / tools" listicle authors to be added (see Tier 2 outreach).
- [ ] Local wedding-vendor directories and association sites.

### Owned profiles (set the canonical link everywhere)
- [ ] TikTok bio → altared.app
- [ ] Instagram bio + link sticker → altared.app
- [ ] YouTube channel "About" + Shorts descriptions → altared.app
- [ ] Pinterest business profile → altared.app (see Pinterest section below)
- [ ] LinkedIn company page

> Social links are mostly nofollow, but they drive referral traffic and branded
> searches, which Google does notice. Worth setting once.

## Tier 2 — Digital PR / expert quotes (highest white-hat ROI)

You have genuine expertise + data on wedding vendor costs. Answer journalist
requests → earn contextual links in real publications (Brides, The Knot, finance sites).

- [ ] **Connectively** (formerly HARO) — https://connectively.us
- [ ] **Featured.com** — https://featured.com
- [ ] **Qwoted** — https://qwoted.com
- [ ] **SourceBottle** — https://sourcebottle.com

Cadence: scan queries 2–3×/week, answer the 1–2 most relevant (wedding budget,
vendor contracts, personal finance, hidden fees). Use the pitch templates below.

- [ ] Build a short **media kit** page (founder bio, 1-line pitch, key stats from the Hidden-Cost Report).

- [x] **2026 Wedding Hidden-Cost Report** — published at `/blog/wedding-hidden-cost-report-2026`.

## Pitch templates

### HARO / Featured — hidden-fee expert quote
> I'm [name], founder of Altared, a tool that helps couples catch hidden wedding
> vendor costs. One fee couples miss most: the venue **service charge** — a
> mandatory 18–24% on food & beverage that is *not* gratuity, so couples often
> tip on top and pay twice. On a $20,000 F&B bill that's $3,600–$4,800 before a
> single tip. The fix: ask in writing whether the service charge includes
> gratuity before signing. [Link: altared.app or the Hidden-Cost Report]

### Outreach — "add us to your round-up"
> Hi [name] — loved your piece on [best wedding budget tools]. We built Altared
> to do one thing those tools don't: pull every vendor's *real* all-in number
> (add-ons, overages, mandatory fees) into one comparison so couples catch hidden
> costs before signing. Would you consider adding it to the list? Happy to share
> our 2026 Wedding Hidden-Cost Report with original fee breakdowns: [link].
